## Stage Cards (the grid)

Six cards, each with a tag line, a one-line description, and the destination tool.

### 0 · SEED
**Frame it.**
You have an idea (or want one). The work right now is sharpening: who, what problem, why you, why now.
→ `/ideate`

### 1 · DISCOVER
**Hear it.**
The idea exists; the customer is hypothetical. Job #1 is hearing the problem in their words, not yours.
→ `/validate`

### 2 · VALIDATE
**Prove it.**
You've talked to people. Now you need money on the table — pre-orders, pilots, deposits. Real signal, not nods.
→ `/validate`

### 3 · BUILD
**Make it.**
You've earned the right to build. The trap: building too much. We help you ship the version you'd defend in public.
→ `/product`

### 4 · LAUNCH
**Sell it.**
A product exists. The constraint is now finding the first customers and learning what works to win them.
→ `/hub` + `/sales`

### 5 · GROW
**Multiply it.**
You have repeat customers and channels that work. Now: more of them, more reliably, without the founder bottleneck.
→ `/hub`, `/sales`, `/customer-success`, `/finance`

### 6 · SCALE
**Operate it.**
You've earned product-market fit. The constraint is now operations, hiring, capital. This is where we stop adding value — here's where to go next.
→ Graduation

## Footer voice / tagline candidates

Three options for a one-line tagline; pick one or split-test:

1. *Tools, not theory.*
2. *Next step. Every time.*
3. *Built for the founder doing the work, not reading about it.*

My favorite: **#3.** It sets expectation (this is for doers), excludes the audience we don't want (people who collect books), and respects the reader.

## Voice rules (for everything across these tools)

- Verbs > adjectives. "Ship the worst version" not "consider an MVP approach."
- Specifics > abstractions. "Cold outreach reply rate under 2%" not "low engagement."
- Negatives carry weight. "Don't hire reps before you have a motion" lands harder than "consider timing."
- No "we get it" / "we know it's hard" empathy filler. Earn empathy by being useful.
- No emojis. No "💡 Pro tip:" callouts. No exclamation marks (one or two per page max).
- One opinion per paragraph. If you can't defend it, cut it.
- Refuse questions you can't answer well. "Should I incorporate as an LLC?" → "Depends. Here's the 3-question tree." Never: "Great question!"

## Microcopy: buttons, headers, prompts

| Primary CTA on quiz | **Take the 1-minute quiz** | "Start your journey" |

| Disclaimer for /finance | **General guidance. Not legal or tax advice.** | "Please consult a professional before acting on any of this content blah blah." |
