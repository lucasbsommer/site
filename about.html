I've been building things since my dorm room.

I'm Lucas Sommer — a serial entrepreneur from Newberg, Oregon. I build companies, write about the entrepreneurial mindset, and try to make useful things for people who are building too.

I've started companies in music, marketing, luxury fashion, fintech, construction, agriculture, and AI. Some worked. Some didn't. All of them taught me something I couldn't have learned any other way.

The Short Version

Valedictorian in a small Oregon town. Full academic scholarship to the University of Miami studying finance and music. Built a recording studio in my dorm room. Won the Budgetball championship. Became the first employee at The Launch Pad, U of Miami's entrepreneurship department, where I consulted 400+ student businesses. Won the WeMedia Pitch It! Contest ($25K prize) for Audimated and got featured on NBC News, Entrepreneur.com, and Forbes. Started a marketing agency in a spare bedroom with $1,000 and grew it to 13 employees before it was acquired. Ran operations for Del Toro Shoes in Miami. Led marketing for LeadsRx (attribution SaaS) and guest lectured at Georgetown.

Today I run Ad-Apt (marketing agency, 15+ years), Boss PDX (construction), Grove Vineyard (Willamette Valley Pinot Noir), IntraAlpha (algo trading), and I'm building Onit — an AI-powered marketing platform. I'm also writing a book called Monkey Business about why your brain is wired to fail at entrepreneurship.

How I Think

Assets over income

Everything I do should trade time, expertise, and energy for scarce, rare assets. Infrastructure is an asset.

Time is the scarcest resource

Tasks and goals should be organized by their ability to save my time or save others theirs. Time-saving infrastructure.

Execution is everything

Ideas are the easy part. Writing a business plan is not action. Planning is not doing. What are you going to do about it?

Seek the margin

Don't find the hardest problem — find the biggest gap between what the market pays and what it costs you to deliver.
