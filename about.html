I've been building things since my dorm room.

I'm Lucas Sommer — a serial entrepreneur from Newberg, Oregon. I build companies, write about the entrepreneurial mindset, and try to make useful things for people who are building too.

I've started companies in music, marketing, luxury fashion, fintech, construction, agriculture, and AI. Some worked. Some didn't. All of them taught me something I couldn't have learned any other way.

The Short Version

Valedictorian in a small Oregon town. Full academic scholarship to the University of Miami studying finance and music. Built four music companies from my dorm room. Won the Budgetball championship. First employee at The Launch Pad, where I consulted 400+ student businesses. Won WeMedia Pitch It! ($25K) for Audimated — featured on NBC News, Entrepreneur.com, and Forbes. Built Socialated from $1,000 to 13 employees before it was acquired. Formed Lucas Sommer Inc., merged it with my partner Bryan Lozano's company to create Ad-Apt — where we've delivered results for Netflix, Payless (400% ROAS), Teton Gravity Research (7.2x ROAS), Circle K, and Benchmade. Ran operations at Del Toro Shoes. Led marketing at LeadsRx (acquired by Unbounce) and guest lectured at Georgetown.

Today I run Ad-Apt (marketing agency serving Netflix, Circle K, Benchmade, Payless), Boss PDX (construction in Portland), Halem Heights (Willamette Valley vineyard), and IntraAlpha (algo trading). I'm building Onit — an AI-powered marketing platform — and writing a book called Monkey Business about why your brain is wired to fail at entrepreneurship. LeadsRx, where I ran marketing, was acquired by Unbounce. Socialated, the agency I built from $1K, was acquired by Virsocom. Two companies I helped build got acquired. Now I'm building more.

How I Think

Assets over income

Everything I do should trade time, expertise, and energy for scarce, rare assets. Infrastructure is an asset.

Time is the scarcest resource

Tasks and goals should be organized by their ability to save my time or save others theirs. Time-saving infrastructure.

Execution is everything

Ideas are the easy part. Writing a business plan is not action. Planning is not doing. What are you going to do about it?

Seek the margin

Don't find the hardest problem — find the biggest gap between what the market pays and what it costs you to deliver.
