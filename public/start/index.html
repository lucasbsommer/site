A tool by Lucas Sommer

A clearer path from idea to a working business.

Most "how to start a company" advice is either a textbook or a tweet. This is neither — it's a set of tools that ask where you are, tell you what to do next, and keep you out of the mistakes that kill most ideas.

Where are you?
0
Seed
Frame it.
You have an idea (or want one). The work is sharpening: who, what problem, why you, why now.
→ /ideate
1
Discover
Hear it.
The idea exists; the customer is hypothetical. Job #1 is hearing the problem in their words, not yours.
→ /validate
2
Validate
Prove it.
You've talked to people. Now you need money on the table — pre-orders, pilots, deposits. Real signal, not nods.
→ /validate
3
Build
Make it.
You've earned the right to build. The trap: building too much. Ship the version you'd defend in public.
→ /product
4
Launch
Sell it.
A product exists. The constraint is finding first customers and learning what wins them.
→ /hub + /sales
5
Grow
Multiply it.
You have repeat customers and channels that work. More of them, more reliably, without the founder bottleneck.
→ /hub + /sales
6
Scale
Operate it.
You've earned product-market fit. The constraint is now operations, hiring, capital. Here's where we stop adding value.
→ Graduation
